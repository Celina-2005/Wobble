### Discovery Phase – Problem Exploration and Concept Selection (September – October)
The Discovery Phase combines a divergent and convergent research step with the aim of defining the core problem and establishing an initial product direction.
In September, a **literature review (N=8)** was conducted on stressors in raising young children. The focus was on problem areas such as emotional escalation, parental overload, and self-regulation in young children. This divergent step resulted in a broad overview of potential sources of stress.
In October, a **field study as survey (N=21)** followed with parents of children aged 2 to 5. This convergent step aimed to determine which stressor from the literature review was perceived as most dominant by the target group. The combination of literature and field data led to a well-founded problem definition and the selection of a single central focus. The phase concluded with a concept pitch, presenting the selected product concept.


### Definition Phase – Concept Delineation and Validation (November – January)
The Definition Phase focuses on deepening and testing the chosen concept. In November, a divergent brainstorming phase was initiated based on the design requirements from Discovery. This phase explored key factors, interaction principles, and potential solutions. Parallel to this, test protocols were developed for field research.
In December, **field studies as waves (N=29)** were conducted on emotion skills and emotion recognition in children aged 2 to 5. This convergent phase yielded less relevant data than expected. Therefore, a supplementary, divergent literature review was conducted to fill in the gaps and strengthen the concept's foundation.


### Evolution of Prototypes
Within semester 1, prototyping was limited to **conceptual and low-fidelity explorations**, such as sketches, scenarios, and cardboard models. Physical and interactive prototypes will be further developed and tested in semester 2, building on the insights gained in semester 1.
